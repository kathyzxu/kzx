July, 2024

> July 1

Moved (again) into an apartment complex in East Williamsburg a four minute walk from the L. I'm single and my commute to work is a lot shorter now. I'm staying with this skinny eclectic 50-something-year-old record store owner named Jay, who survives off ginger mango smoothies and a massive post-punk record collection that he likes to break out in the mornings. It's getting almost unbearably hot in New York and bedrotting has become ultra inticing, but at least I've been out and making new friends. And the city is still beautiful.

> July 5

This past week has deadass been the longest week of my life...

FINALLY got my fake and went out to a bunch of bars in the Bushwick area. Being surrounded by people in their mid-20s--it's interesting to see how people transition into adulthood. Semi-adjusted baristas, mini-influencers, "photographers," "creatives"... Everybody is beautiful and broken and a bit aimless.

Yesteray we ran around in the rain downtown after the fireworks had subsided. Noel and I had taken a gram and a half of shrooms--not enough to fully trip, but enough to make the city a bit prettier than it usually is. I'm pretty sure the combination of the weather and the psychedelics was what gave me a cough the next day, but it was worth it. Everything was quiet and beautiful. Grace #2 was having her moment, prancing around the West Village and not saying a word, and I loved it.

I feel like EVERYTHING has happened to me in the past year. Literally even in the span of the past two weeks, so much has changed. What's terrifying is that I'm already feeling a bit sick of all the new things I've experienced. I've gone out so much and met and laughed with so many new people, but there's always a tinge of loneliness undercutting every interaction.

I don't know what will fix that loneliness. I don't want a relationship, I don't need more friends. I miss the people at home, but when I go back it's not the same. Plus we're never all in the same place at the same time anymore. I still feel like a kid but I can see my body changing. When I look in the mirror, I see more of a woman than a girl.
